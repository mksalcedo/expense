Yes, both Monarch and Simplifi are designed to **automatically import transactions** once you've connected your accounts.

Typically:

- You connect your bank, credit cards, mortgage, investment accounts, etc. once.

- Some banks update almost instantly; others may lag by a day or two.
- Occasionally a connection breaks because a bank changes its login process, and you'll need to re-authenticate.
- Some smaller credit unions don't connect as reliably as major banks.
- Investment accounts sometimes update less frequently than checking and credit card accounts.

- Tax, where available
- Refund status
- Shipment or charge information

Known products would use the product-category lookup table.

Unknown products would appear in a small review queue.

| Product | Suggested category | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Doctor’s Best Magnesium | Supplements | Approve |
| Organic almond flour | Groceries | Approve |
| USB cable | Miscellaneous | Approve |

Because most of your products recur, the review queue should become very small after the initial setup.

## 5. Amazon charge matching

This is the difficult part.

Amazon may:

- Split one order into several Amex charges
- Combine items based on shipment
- Apply Subscribe & Save discounts
- Apply gift-card funds
- Issue partial refunds
- Charge tax at the shipment level

Therefore, we should not force every item to match one bank transaction immediately.

I recommend storing two separate layers:

### Bank transaction

```text
AMAZON MKTPLACE
$86.47
Posted July 14
```

### Amazon purchase items

```text
Magnesium       $24.99
Fish oil        $31.49
Almond flour    $19.99
Tax/adjustment   $10.00
```

The system then creates a **reconciliation group** linking one or more Amazon charges to one or more Amazon orders or shipments.

It should show:

```text
Bank charges total:     $86.47
Amazon item total:      $86.47
Difference:              $0.00
Status:                  Matched
```

Small unresolved differences could be assigned to tax or rounding. Larger differences would remain in review.

## 6. Current spending totals

Once categorized, the same transaction history supports all your spending views:

```text
Groceries spent this week =
    Checking grocery purchases
  + Amex grocery purchases
  + Grocery portions of mixed transactions
  + Grocery Amazon items
  - Grocery refunds
```

That gives you accurate totals several times during the week without entering receipts.

## 7. Amex payment correlation

Amex purchases affect two separate areas:

### On the purchase date

They count immediately against the relevant spending budget.

### On the planned payment date

They increase the amount that must come from checking.

Your Amex payment forecast becomes:

```text
Qualifying new Amex charges
+ $1,100 planned principal reduction
= Planned checking payment to Amex
```

We should assign Amex purchases to a statement cycle using their **posted date**, statement closing date, and payment due date.

The app should show each planned payment’s composition:

| Payment component | Amount |
|---|---:|
| New grocery purchases | $1,050 |
| New supplement purchases | $720 |
| Restaurants and gas | $230 |
| Extra debt reduction | $1,100 |
| **Total planned payment** | **$3,100** |
